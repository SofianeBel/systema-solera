# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Systema Solera is for a broad tech audience discovering the pre-public GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna model family through a memorable launch surface rather than a conventional pricing table. The visitor may be a developer, a founder, a designer, or simply someone curious about AI model positioning. They arrive with low context and should understand the hierarchy quickly: Sol is the flagship, Terra is the balanced everyday model, and Luna is the fast high-volume model.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Sol, Terra, and Luna as a celestial system. Its job is to make the model lineup feel tangible: three bodies in one orbit, each with a clear role, price posture, and atmosphere. Success means a visitor remembers the names, understands the relative positioning, and feels the card-to-space transition as the signature interaction.

## Brand Personality

Precise, cinematic, gravitational. The brand should feel like a quiet launch from a future AI lab: minimal in typography, vast in space, and technically confident without becoming cold or academic.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS grammar: purple gradients, badge stacks, fake metrics, template feature grids, and ornamental cards with no physical logic. Avoid a sterile NASA simulator that feels like scientific software instead of a premium campaign. Avoid visual ambition that breaks mobile performance or makes the experience unusable on average laptops.

## Design Principles

- Make scale the signature: the cards already contain 3D bodies, but the framing should make them feel too immense to fully perceive.
- Show before explaining: the visitor should feel Sol, Terra, and Luna through light, orbit, material, and motion before reading the copy.
- Use OpenAI-like restraint without copying marks: precise neutral typography, clean pricing language, and calm controls.
- Keep one cinematic idea per state: launch grid, selected body, and detail overlays should not compete.
- Preserve performance as craft: shader quality and motion are only successful when interaction remains fluid.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls. Every transition must respect reduced motion with a crossfade or instant scene swap. Keyboard users must be able to select each model card and return from the immersive scene. Mobile users should receive the same narrative with reduced geometry, texture resolution, and particle density when needed.
